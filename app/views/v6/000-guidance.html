Property documents

Different documents will give you different information. You only need to buy the documents you need. For example, you can choose to buy the title plan and deeds or just the title register.

Title register (official copy)

The title register has details about the property or land. It includes:

  • the title number
  • who owns it
  • what they paid for it (properties only, if available)
  • any rights of way
  • whether the property is freehold or leasehold (known as ‘tenure’)
  • the lender’s name and address (if there is a mortgage on the property)
  • whether a mortgage on it has been ‘discharged’, for example paid off

You can download PDF or view an online copy of the title register. The online version is not an official copy.

Title plan (official copy)

The title plan is a map showing the:

  • property’s location
  • general boundaries

There's usually no record of:

  • the exact boundary between two properties
  • who owns the hedge, wall, tree or fence between 2 properties

Some title plans will show other information referred to in the title register. For example rights of way, utilities equipment positions or areas affected by restrictive covenants.

You can download a PDF of the title plan (official copy).

Find out more about title plans.

Copy of title deeds

Copies of the deeds can give you:

  • current and past information about a registered property, such as its previous owners
  • more details about rights over adjoining land

You can download a zip folder of the deeds.

Copies of deeds cannot be used to prove ownership, for example in a court case. Get an official copy of the title register instead.
