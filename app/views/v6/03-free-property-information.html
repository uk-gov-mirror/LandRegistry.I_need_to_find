What's freehold?

You own a freehold property until you sell it, give it away or it otherwise passes to someone else.

Most houses are freehold. Most flats are leasehold. That means someone else owns the freehold for the building the flat is in.

If you want to know who owns a particular flat, you probably need the leasehold instead.

Houses can also be leasehold and usually are if they’re bought through a shared ownership scheme.

Things you can and cannot do with the property

Easements

Easements are rights that the owner of one piece of land has over another piece of land. For example, rights of way.

There are easements recorded in the register - to view details you'll need to buy the title register.

Restrictive covenants

Restrictive covenants are contracts between two landowners- one landowner promises not not to carry out particular acts on the other's land.

There are no restrictive covenants recorded in the register. There may be some that have not been recorded.
