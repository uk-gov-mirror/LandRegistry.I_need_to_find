What's commonhold?

Commonhold is a way of owning an individual property that is part of a bigger property (for example, a flat in a block).

It is an alternative to leasehold.

Each individual unit (or flat) is exclusively owned by a unit-holder and the common areas (for example, stairways and corridors) are owned by a commonhold association.

A commonhold association is a company. Each unit-holder is a member of the commonhold association.
