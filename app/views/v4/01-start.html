Use this service to get information about a property in England and Wales, even if you don't own it.

Free information

HM Land Registry holds records about most property or land sold in England or Wales since 1993. We can give you some of this information for free.

You can:

  • print a copy
  • download a copy
  • forward a copy to an email address
What information can I get for free?

You can view a summary of property information including:

  • the full address
  • price paid history- you won't get this if it was bought before April 2000, if less than £100 was paid or stated, or if HM Land Registry have agreed not to record the price
  • general boundaries (title extent)
  • property price trends for the local area
  • details of any planning applications
  • if there are any easements- a right that the owner of one piece of land has over another piece of land
  • if there are any restrictive covenants- contracts between two landowners: one landowner promises the other landowner not to carry out particular acts on their land

We may have more information about properties you're searching for if it's been recorded in the register.

Premium information

You'll need an email address and debit or credit card for premium information.

We can give you a copy of the:

  • title register (£3)
    • What's this?

    The title register has details about the property or land. It includes:

    • the title number
    • who owns it
    • what they paid for it (properties only, if available)
    • any rights of way
    • whether a mortgage on it has been ‘discharged’, for example paid off
  • title plan (£3)
    • What's this?

    The title plan is a map showing:

    • the property's location
    • the general boundaries - there’s usually no record of exact boundaries
  • property deeds (£3)
    • What this?
    You'll get a copy of the deeds referred to in the register. Copies of deeds can’t be used to prove ownership, for example in a court case.
