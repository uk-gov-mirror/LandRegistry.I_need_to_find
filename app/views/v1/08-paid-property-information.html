What's freehold?

You own a freehold building and land outright. It's yours until you sell it or give it away.

Most houses are freehold. Most flats are leasehold. That means someone else owns the freehold for the building the flat is in.

If you want to know who owns a particular flat, you probably need the leasehold instead.

Houses can also be leasehold and usually are if they’re bought through a shared ownership scheme.

Restrictive covenants

Restrictive covenants are covenants that impose a restriction on the use of land so that the value and enjoyment of adjoining land will be preserved.

  • The premises and all buildings thereon shall be used and occupied solely and strictly as a private residence.

  • No washing (whether sheets blankets garments or other articles of any kind) shall be hung up to dry or otherwise exposed to view on any portion of the land hereby demised whether on clothes lines or from the windows of the house or otherwise howsoever.
